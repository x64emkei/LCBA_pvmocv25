History of LCBA

Learn and know the rich history and journey of the Laguna College of Business and Arts, from its humble beginnings to its present status as a cornerstone of education in the region.


Don Pedro E. Llamas (Portrait)

Don Pedro E. Llamas (Half-Bust Statue)

The Founder

Don Pedro E. Llamas (1884–1946), a pioneering educator and public servant from Pagsanjan, Laguna, founded the institution. An alumnus of the University of Michigan, he first established Pagsanjan Academy in 1923. Recognizing the need for a secondary school in Calamba, he then founded the Laguna Institute in 1930.

Laguna Institute Logo

The Laguna Institute (L.I.)

The Laguna Institute formally opened in June 1930 with 95 students. The first campus was a rented two-story building on Burgos Street. By 1933, it was officially recognized by the government. After a pause during World War II, the school reopened and expanded in 1950, adding elementary and college departments.

LCBA Official Logo

The Name Change to LCBA

On its 50th anniversary in 1980, the institution officially adopted the name Laguna College of Business and Arts (LCBA) to reflect its expanded role and commitment to higher education in both business and the arts.
